## The System of Record Problem

Every function needs a system of record—a single source of truth. Sales has the CRM. Finance has the ERP. HR has the HRIS. But what about marketing campaigns?

Most marketing teams default to their CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce) or a project management tool (Asana, Monday). Neither was designed for campaign management.

## Why Your CRM Isn't Enough

CRMs are optimized for tracking contacts, companies, and deals—not campaigns.

### What CRMs Do Well

- Contact and company records
- Deal pipeline tracking
- Activity logging
- Basic campaign association

### What CRMs Don't Do

- **Campaign planning** — Brief creation, timeline management
- **Approval workflows** — Multi-stakeholder sign-off
- **Budget tracking** — Spend vs. plan at the campaign level
- **Cross-channel performance** — Unified view of email + ads + events
- **Real-time ROI** — Campaign-level revenue attribution

## Why Project Management Tools Fall Short

Tools like Asana, Monday, and Notion are great for task management but lack:

- **Marketing-specific fields** — Budget, target audience, KPIs
- **CRM integration** — Connecting campaigns to pipeline
- **Attribution data** — Linking campaigns to revenue

## What a Campaign System of Record Provides

A true campaign system of record offers:

1. **Campaign registry** — Every campaign in one place
2. **Standardized fields** — Goals, audience, budget, timeline, KPIs
3. **Workflow management** — Planning, approval, execution stages
4. **Performance tracking** — Metrics across all channels
5. **Revenue attribution** — Connection to pipeline and closed-won
